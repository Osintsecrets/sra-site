How it works

The DARK Threat Model: a simple, transparent process that surfaces what matters.

Consent & Transparency

Before anything begins, you’ll get a clear explanation and sign a consent form. We proceed only when you confirm you understand the scope.

  • Open Source Intelligence only
  • No login, no password requests
  • No attempts to bypass privacy
  • Public-view exactly as a stranger

Discover

We review your public Facebook like a stranger would and identify high-risk data points (posts, photos, tags, About fields, likes, groups, events).

We don’t list everything—only what a bad actor would target first.

Analyze

Each data point is assessed for realistic misuse (impersonation, harassment, social engineering, stalking). We include a fictional yet plausible threat simulation.

Reduce & Keep Safe

You receive a Facebook Privacy Guide and a long-term Keep Safe guide. They show options—you decide what to keep public or change.

What you get

Clear documentation, screenshot evidence, and practical guidance—without tech jargon.

Ethics & Consent

This service exists to protect, educate, and empower—not to exploit.

  • I only audit Facebook accounts with signed consent from the owner.
  • No audits on behalf of spouses, partners, or third parties.
  • Open Source Intelligence only—publicly available info.
  • I do not login, friend, or connect with you.
  • I do not attempt to bypass privacy or protections.

You’ll know exactly what I’ll do before I begin. Every audit starts with transparency, consent, and accountability.

Why this matters

Online exposure can enable impersonation, harassment, doxxing, and targeted scams. Clarity helps you choose your level of openness.
